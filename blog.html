Top 10 Essential Tips for Monster Survivors Beginners

New to Monster Survivors? These essential tips will help you survive longer, level up faster, and master the game's mechanics. Whether you're just starting out or looking to improve your skills, these strategies will give you the edge against the endless monster hordes.

1. Choose the Right Character for Your Playstyle

Different characters have unique strengths and starting weapons. For beginners, we recommend:

  • Guardian: High health and defense, perfect for learning enemy patterns
  • Archer: Long-range attacks help keep monsters at a distance
  • Knight: Balanced stats and a reliable starting weapon

Avoid characters like the Warlock or Assassin until you're more experienced, as they have lower health pools and require more skill to use effectively.

2. Movement is Key to Survival

In Monster Survivors, standing still means certain death. Always keep moving to:

  • Avoid being surrounded by enemies
  • Kite large groups to manage them more effectively
  • Collect experience gems and power-ups

Try moving in large circles or figure-eight patterns to keep enemies grouped together, making them easier to hit with area-of-effect weapons.

3. Prioritize the Right Upgrades

When leveling up, choose upgrades in this order of priority:

  1. Weapons that complement your character's strengths
  2. Area-of-effect (AoE) weapons for crowd control
  3. Movement speed increases
  4. Health and armor upgrades
  5. Cooldown reduction

Remember that it's generally better to fully upgrade a few weapons than to have many at low levels.

4. Learn Enemy Patterns

Different monsters have distinct movement and attack patterns:

  • Slimes: Slow but can appear in large numbers
  • Wolves: Fast and attack in packs
  • Mages: Fire projectiles from a distance
  • Brutes: Slow but deal high damage when they hit

Study these patterns and adjust your movement strategy accordingly.

5. Balance Your Weapon Selection

A balanced arsenal is crucial for success:

  • Include at least one area-of-effect weapon (like Tornado or Lightning Ring)
  • Have a reliable single-target weapon (like Magic Dagger or Holy Sword)
  • Consider one defensive option (Shield or Garlic Aura)

Some weapons have synergy effects when paired together, so experiment with different combinations.

6. Don't Rush to Collect Everything

When experience gems and items drop, don't immediately rush to collect them if it puts you in danger. Instead:

  • Clear nearby enemies first
  • Use magnet items to attract gems from a distance
  • Plan collection routes that avoid dense enemy clusters

Experience gems remain on the field for quite some time, so prioritize your safety.

Pro Tip: The 5-Minute Rule

The game difficulty increases significantly at the 5-minute mark and again at 10 minutes. Make sure you're prepared with upgraded weapons before these difficulty spikes.

7. Use Environmental Features

Maps contain various features that can help or hinder your survival:

  • Use obstacles to funnel enemies into narrow paths
  • Avoid corners where you can get trapped
  • Look for healing shrines and treasure chests

Learning to use the environment effectively can be the difference between a short run and a new high score.

8. Don't Ignore Passive Items

While weapons get most of the attention, passive items provide crucial bonuses:

  • Spinach: Increases damage output
  • Armor: Reduces damage taken
  • Wings: Boosts movement speed
  • Empty Tome: Reduces cooldowns
  • Candelabra: Increases area of effect

A well-rounded set of passive items can dramatically increase your survival chances.

9. Watch Your Level Progress

Keep an eye on your experience bar and plan ahead:

  • If you're close to leveling up, consider holding off on picking up health if you're not in immediate danger
  • Try to level up before facing elite enemies or bosses
  • Know which upgrades you want before leveling to make quick decisions

Smart experience management helps maintain momentum throughout your run.

10. Learn from Each Run

Every game, successful or not, is a learning opportunity:

  • Analyze what led to your defeat
  • Try different weapon combinations
  • Experiment with various characters and builds
  • Set specific goals for each run (e.g., reach a certain time, try a new character)

The more you play, the better you'll understand the game's mechanics and how to use them to your advantage.

Conclusion
